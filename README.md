# Rocketry Training – Aerospace Simulation Workshop Project

## Overview

This project focuses on the structural and aerodynamic analysis of a rocket fin using engineering simulation techniques. The study was conducted using SimScale to understand the behavior of a rocket fin under applied loads and aerodynamic conditions.

The project demonstrates the application of Finite Element Method (FEM) and Computational Fluid Dynamics (CFD) concepts in aerospace engineering.

## Objectives

* Analyze the structural behavior of a rocket fin.
* Evaluate stress distribution and deformation characteristics.
* Study aerodynamic effects on the fin.
* Understand the importance of meshing and boundary conditions.
* Calculate the factor of safety for the design.

## Tools Used

* SimScale
* Finite Element Method (FEM)
* Computational Fluid Dynamics (CFD)

## Results

The analysis indicates that the rocket fin can withstand the assumed loading conditions while maintaining structural integrity. Stress concentrations were observed near the support region, and deformation remained within acceptable limits. The study demonstrates the effectiveness of simulation-driven design approaches in aerospace applications.
